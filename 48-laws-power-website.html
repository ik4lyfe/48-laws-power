The Philosophy of Power

To some, power is a dirty word. But history teaches us a different lesson. The laws of power are not a construct of modern ambition, but historical observations of human behavior, drawn from the strategies of Machiavelli, Sun Tzu, and Casanova.

Whether you are aiming for conquest, self-defense, or simply trying to understand the machinations of those around you, observing these laws is essential. To ignore them is to leave yourself vulnerable in a world that respects only strength and cunning.

"Lord, protect me from my friends; I can take care of my enemies."

— Voltaire

The Heavy Hitters

Foundational Strategies

01

Never Outshine the Master

Always make those above you feel comfortably superior. In your desire to please or impress them, do not go too far in displaying your talents or you might accomplish the opposite—inspire fear and insecurity.

Key Transgression:

Nicolas Fouquet outshone King Louis XIV with a lavish party, resulting in a lifetime of imprisonment.

15

Crush Your Enemy Totally

All great leaders since Moses have known that a feared enemy must be crushed completely. If one ember is left alight, no matter how dimly it smolders, a fire will eventually break out.

The Principle:

An enemy left partially destroyed will seek revenge. Annihilate them in body and spirit.

48

Assume Formlessness

By taking a shape, by having a visible plan, you open yourself to attack. Instead of taking a form for your enemy to grasp, keep yourself adaptable and on the move. Accept that nothing is certain.

The Ultimate Defense:

Like water, protect yourself by adapting to whatever receptacle you are poured into.
